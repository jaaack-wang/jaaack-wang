LLMs can Perform Multi-Dimensional Analytic Writing Assessments: A Case Study of L2 Graduate-Level Academic English Writing

Zhengxiang Wang, Veronika Makarova, Zhi Li, Jordan Kodner, Owen Rambow
ACL 2025 (Main)
Paper Code

Abstract

This work studies whether modern LLMs can provide reliable multi-dimensional analytic writing assessments for graduate-level L2 academic English writing. The paper introduces a corpus of 141 annotated literature reviews and presents ProEval, an evaluation framework for analyzing the quality of generated feedback comments. Together, the corpus and framework enable systematic analysis of rubric-based writing assessment quality across multiple analytic dimensions.

Overview

The project contributes a previously unpublished L2 graduate writing corpus with human analytic assessments and an open implementation of ProEval. This setup supports reproducible comparison of feedback-generation quality and assessment consistency.

ACL 2025 paper on multi-dimensional analytic writing assessment with LLMs.

Conclusion

The paper shows how corpus construction plus targeted automatic evaluation can support more rigorous study of LLM-based writing assessment. It also provides practical resources for future work on educational NLP, L2 writing feedback, and reliable rubric-based assessment.
