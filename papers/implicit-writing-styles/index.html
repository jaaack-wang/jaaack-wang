Catch Me If You Can? Not Yet: LLMs Still Struggle to Imitate the Implicit Writing Styles of Everyday Authors

Zhengxiang Wang*, Nafis Irtiza Tripto*, Solha Park, Zhenzhen Li, Jiawei Zhou
EMNLP 2025 (Findings)
Paper Code arXiv

Abstract

As large language models become increasingly integrated into personal writing tools, a central question is whether they can faithfully imitate an individual's writing style from only a few examples. We present a comprehensive evaluation of state-of-the-art LLMs on implicit personal style imitation via in-context learning. We use an ensemble of complementary metrics including authorship attribution, authorship verification, style matching, and AI detection, with over 40,000 generations per model across news, email, forums, and blogs from more than 400 real-world authors. Results show that while models can approximate style in structured formats, they still struggle with nuanced and informal personal writing styles.

Main Results

LLMs perform better on structured genres and remain weak on nuanced informal writing styles.

Conclusion

Current LLMs do not yet provide reliable personalization for implicit user writing style, especially in informal and nuanced domains. The results reveal a fundamental gap between surface-level stylistic matching and faithful style-consistent generation, indicating the need for stronger adaptation methods for robust, user-aligned writing assistance.
