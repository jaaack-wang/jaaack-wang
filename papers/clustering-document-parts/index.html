ICGI 2023

Clustering Document Parts: Detecting and Characterizing Influence Campaigns from Documents

Zhengxiang Wang, Owen Rambow
NLP+CSS @ NAACL 2024
Paper Code

Abstract

We propose a pipeline that detects influence campaigns by clustering document parts, classifying high-influence clusters, and projecting cluster decisions back to documents. Unlike direct document-level classification, this framework isolates the specific spans that drive campaign signals and supports interpretable characterization of coordinated influence behavior. The method combines non-lexical linguistic features, SBERT-based clustering, and aggregation over many clustering runs to improve robustness.

Main Results

The pipeline improves document-level campaign detection substantially over baselines. With XGBoost and sentence-level clustering plus aggregation, the best reported F1 reaches 77.8 (precision 86.5, recall 70.7), compared with 50.7 F1 for direct document classification and 43.8 F1 for document-level clustering baselines.

Overview of the clustering-based influence-campaign detection pipeline.

Aggregating high-influence clusters across many clustering configurations consistently improves F1 versus averaging single clustering runs. The gain is strongest when combined with XGBoost cluster classification, yielding higher recall while preserving strong precision in the final document predictions.

Aggregation versus no aggregation with XGBoost for high-influence cluster classification.

Conclusion

Clustering document parts before document classification offers a stronger and more interpretable route for influence-campaign analysis than direct document-level approaches. The approach better captures campaign-related signals that occur in only parts of long, heterogeneous documents, and cluster aggregation further stabilizes performance across clustering choices. The framework also opens a path toward finer-grained campaign characterization beyond binary detection.
